Ten Thousand Villages

Project Overview

Mark

Ten Thousand Villages of Austin is a non-profit organization, whose central mission is to be a conduit for artisans to share their talents with the world. They envision a world were artisans in undeveloped countries receive fair wages for their goods. How might we redesign the website to support the vision and mission of this organization.

Research

Our research started with recruiting friends, family and coworkers to complete a short survey. The results revealed that the majority of those surveyed were not well versed in sustainable shopping. However, the majority were interested in learning about sustainable goods. More importantly, most people were interested in supporting social impact initiatives through shopping. And the other large majority were had not ruled it out.

Ideation

Before we could start the redesign we met with a five shoppers to identify the pain points they felt while using the Ten Thousand villages’ website.  In our initial assessment, we had a good idea of what some of those pain points might be. It became very evident during out testing that the shoppers felt them too.We began the practice of designing our interface with a heuristic evaluation of the current website. This analysis would help to inform our process moving forward. We found there were several key areas that required improvement including creating a stronger sense of hierarchy, and highlighting actionable items like donating or volunteering.
